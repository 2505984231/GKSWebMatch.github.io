Tianjin Port

Introduction

Tianjin Port, also known as Tianjin Xingang, is located at the mouth of the Haihe River in Tianjin, the People's Republic of China. It is at the intersection of the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei urban agglomeration and the Bohai Economic Circle. It is an important comprehensive port and foreign trade port in northern China. Tianjin Port is the artificial deep-water port with the highest level of world waterway built on the muddy shoal and built by the port. At present, the main channel of Tianjin Port has a water depth of 21 meters, which can meet the import and export of 300,000-ton crude oil ships and the most advanced container ships in the world.

In 2013, the cargo throughput of Tianjin Port exceeded 500 million tons for the first time, and the container throughput exceeded 13 million TEUs, making it the first 500 million tons of ports in northern China. On January 1, 2014, China's first artificially excavated retest channel was officially opened in Tianjin Port. On March 4, 2015, Tianjin will invest 16 billion yuan to build Tianjin Port. On April 2, 2016, it was learned from Tianjin Port Group that the Tianjin Port Nanjiang No. 27 General Wharf Project, which was listed as one of the key construction projects in Tianjin in 2016, started construction. After the completion of the project, it will play an important role in realizing the Tianjin Port's “Northern Mine South Shift” strategy and the “East High-end, South-quality, North-up” overall layout and functional positioning, and enhance its external service capabilities. The total investment of the project is 1.41 billion yuan, and the construction of a vacant cargo space of 9.8 million tons with a capacity of 9.8 million tons and a dock with a total area of about 251,500 square meters.

Tianjin Port

history
The history of Tianjin Port can be traced back to the Han Dynasty and has formed a seaport since the Tang Dynasty. It was officially opened to the outside world in 1860 and is one of the earliest ports for foreign trade in China. Tianjin Xingang was founded in 1939. After three years of restoration, it was reopened on October 17, 1952. In 2009, Tianjin Port achieved a higher penetration rate. As of 18:00 on December 30, the cargo throughput exceeded 380 million tons, and the container completed 8.7 million TEUs. The two indicators increased by 6.7% and 2.4% respectively, and the cargo throughput ranked first among the northern ports.
business
Tianjin Port's main business includes: investment in ports with its own funds: land development in port areas; loading and unloading; warehousing; cargo transit and distribution, port tally; passenger and cargo transportation services; barge; ship guidance and related services; Lease and maintenance of port facilities, equipment and port machinery; commodity packaging and processing; construction of industrial equipment; supply and marketing of materials; oil supply and water supply for ships and cargo-level ships. Labor dispatching of workers at ports in the port (limited to domestic).
Geographical advantage
Tianjin Port is an important waterway transportation hub in North China, Northwest China and Beijing-Tianjin region. The external traffic is very developed, and a large-scale three-dimensional traffic collection and distribution system has been formed. It has three railway lines of Jingha, Beijing-Shanghai, Beijing-Tianjin, and is connected here, and is connected to Beijing-Guangzhou, Beijing-Kowloon, Jingbao, Jingcheng, Jingtong, Jingyu, Shide, Shitai, Bohai, Baolan and Lanxin. The trunk line is connected to the national railway. North Beijing, Inner Mongolia and Northeast China, south to East China and South China, west to the west and northwest inland, and then to Mongolia, Russia and Europe.
