Shanghai Port awarded Saab Ship Safety Contract

Logistics Baba was informed that Saab has received an order from the China Maritime Safety Administration to update and expand Shanghai's Ship Traffic Management Information System (VTMIS) to improve shipping safety and efficiency in the world's largest container port.

The project will take 18 months to complete, including the replacement of most existing radar and communications systems, two transportation centers and the delivery of a new monitoring center.

Saab's expertise in upgrading complex existing systems is a key factor for China Maritime Safety Administration to consider when operating interference is minimal.

Every day, import and export goods worth $3 billion, port users need to continue safe and efficient transportation, which is what Saab can do.

The initial system established by Saab in 2005 was to protect the 33-kilometer East China Sea Bridge connecting Yangshan Deepwater Port and mainland China. Saab will now extend the system to the Wusong area.

At any time, more than 10,000 vessels sailed in the Shanghai Port area. Their actions are often affected by the dense fog of the area. Without Saab's VTMIS, the impact of bad weather will be much greater.

Saab is a leader in providing secure, efficient and easy-to-configure solutions for marine geography, navigation, search and rescue, coastal surveillance, port security and traffic management.

Saab's solutions are deployed in more than 100 ports, including eight of the world's top 20 container ports.

Saab has more than 70 operating transportation centers and tracks more than 50,000 vessels in several ports in Hong Kong, Rotterdam, Shanghai and Australia.
