Qingdao Port

Introduction

Qingdao Port is the port of Qingdao, Shandong Province, China. It is located on the bank of Jiaozhou Bay in Shandong Peninsula. It borders the Yellow Sea and faces Japan and the Korean Peninsula across the sea. It is an international trading port and transit hub for China along the Yellow River Basin and the western coast of the Pacific Rim. In 1892, the Qing government built two docks in Qingdao Bay. In 1901, Qingdao began to build Dagang. In 1976, Qingdao Port began its first container business. At the beginning of 1992, Qingdao Port opened the first international shipping trunk line and docked the first third-generation container ship. On February 24, 2002, Qingdao Port opened to the West Asia International Container Line.

According to the comprehensive information in 2004, the port area of Qingdao Port is 420 square kilometers. Qingdao Port is divided into four parts: Dagang Port Area, Zhonggang Port Area, Huangdao Oil Port Port Area and Qianwan Port Port Area. Qingdao Port business covers Shandong, radiating along the Yellow River and reaching Central Asia. As of 2011, Qingdao Port has 18 terminals and 69 berths. By the end of 2018, Qingdao Port had more than 160 container routes. In 2017, Qingdao Port's cargo throughput exceeded 510 million tons, of which container throughput exceeded 18.31 million TEU. In 2018, Qingdao Port completed an operation container volume of 1.154 million TEU.

Qingdao Port

history
In the thirteenth year of Ming Yongle (1415), the Jiaolai Canal was stopped, but the southern section of the canal was still open to traffic and was transferred to Dengzhou by land. At this time, the status of Banqiao Town Port was replaced by Tatun. After the Ming court approved Xu Wei’s "Local Matters Argument", Qingdao Port, Nuqinkou and Jinkou Ports gradually emerged and became a sea trading port. Since 2016, 10 berths of 6 terminals including Qingdao Port Dongjiakou Port General Terminal and 450,000 tons of crude oil terminal have been officially put into operation.
business
Qingdao Port is mainly engaged in the loading and unloading services of various import and export goods such as containers, coal, crude oil, iron ore and grain, as well as international and domestic passenger services. The ore loading and unloading operations of Qingdao Port are mainly concentrated in the former Hong Kong branch and Dongjiakou branch, providing logistics services such as ore unloading, stacking, storage, and transportation; mixed mining and screening operations; bonded logistics business.
Geographical advantage
Qingdao Port is divided into two parts: waters and land. Waters: From the Taiping Point to the mouth of the mouth, from the corner of the mountain to the mouth of Huangshan, from the mouth of Huangshan to the mouth of the wave, from the mouth of the wave to the foot of the stone mouth, each lead straight line, four companies The in-line waters are the water port boundary. Land: From the tip of Xiaogang South Breakwater, along the west side of Xiaogang, via Heze Road, Jixian Road and Xiaogangyanyi Road to Xiaogang North Breakwater, and then along the China-Hong Kong coastline through Xinjiang Road to Dagang Entrance.
