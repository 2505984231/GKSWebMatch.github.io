Biography

● Fan Lichu, born in 1933, Zhenhai, Zhejiang, graduated from the Department of Bridge and Tunnel Engineering of Tongji University in July 1955. He is currently a professor at the School of Civil Engineering of Tongji University, a doctoral tutor, and the deputy director of the State Key Laboratory of Civil Engineering Disaster Prevention. The country has outstanding contributions to experts. He used to be the dean of the School of Structural Engineering of Tongji University. He is currently the chairman of the International Association of International Bridges (IABSE), the alternate member of the Standing Committee of the International Association of Bridges and Structural Engineering, a member of the EASEC International Steering and Executive Committee, and the executive director of the China Civil Engineering Society. , Chairman of the Bridge and Structural Engineering Branch of the China Civil Engineering Society. In 2001, he was elected as an academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.


Historical life

• Born in Shanghai in 1933.


• In September 1951, he was admitted to the Department of Civil Engineering of Shanghai Jiaotong University.


• In September 1952, the national department was adjusted and transferred to the Department of Road and Bridge of Tongji University to study bridge and tunnel engineering.


• Graduated in July 1955, assigned to stay in school. He has served as a teaching assistant and teacher (1955.8-1978.2); lecturer and associate professor, deputy director and director of the Bridge Research Office, and deputy director and chairman of the Civil Engineering Pilot Professional Committee (February 1978-May 1986); Professor, Academic Affairs Deputy Director (May 1986-May 1987); Vice President and Dean of the School of Structural Engineering (June 1987-September 1997). He is currently the deputy director of the Academic Committee of the State Key Laboratory of Civil Engineering Disaster Prevention (September 1997 to present), and concurrently serves as the vice chairman of the China Civil Engineering Society, the alternate member of the International Association of Bridges (IABSE) and the chairman of the Chinese National Group.


• Since 1955, he has been engaged in the teaching and research of bridge engineering. A number of achievements have been made in the study of bridge space calculation theory, lateral load distribution, concrete creep, and performance of prestressed concrete continuous system.


• After 1980, he edited the first and second editions of the "Bridge Engineering" textbook, which was used by 13 colleges and universities nationwide.


• In 2001, he was elected as an academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.


• At the end of the 1970s, after the Tangshan earthquake, Fan Lichu established an academic team and has been insisting for nearly 30 years. For Fan Lichu, he is the academic leader of this team, the overall academic guidance and direction. A generation that grew up in the early 1980s, few people have not read his book, and most of the entire bridge community has read a book called "Bridge Engineering." Academic absolute equality and freedom are the common feelings of Fan Lichu team members. Fan Lichu feels that a team can do a good job, a simple sentence: to play the advantages of others, tolerate the shortcomings of others. Fan Lichu's indifferent fame and fortune also influenced his entire team in a subtle way, so that everyone can maintain a very peaceful attitude and concentrate on doing what they should do.


• In the past 30 years, Fan Lichu led the research team to adhere to the innovative research on earthquake resistance theory and applied technology, and supported the strong technological backbone for the construction of large-span bridges in China! Sutong Yangtze River Bridge, with a span of 1088 meters, is the world's largest cable-stayed bridge. Its 5,000-meter-long approach bridge has a total of 105 piers, and more than 400 sets of "large-tonnage hyperboloid all-steel seismic isolation bearings" have been applied, directly saving the project cost of about 390 million yuan. This shock-absorbing bearing is the product developed by the Fan Lichu team.


• On January 11, 2010, the “2009 China National Science and Technology Awards Conference” was held in the Great Hall of the People in Beijing. The "Key Technology for Seismic Design of Large Span and High Pier Bridges" by Academician Fan Lichu of Tongji University won the first prize of National Science and Technology Progress Award. The result has been adopted by the national industry standard "Code for Seismic Design of Highway Bridges" and applied to (Shanghai's largest span arch bridge) Shanghai Lupu Bridge, (the first domestic sea-crossing bridge) Donghai Bridge, (the world's largest span double-layered cable-stayed Bridge) Wuhan Tianxingzhou Gongtie Dual-purpose Bridge and other more than 30 national major bridge projects have broken through the key technical problems of economically and reasonably ensuring the seismic safety of the bridge.
