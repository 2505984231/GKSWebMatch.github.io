Dalian Port welcomes the largest cruise ship "Selina"

The picture shows "Selina"


Zhongxin.com, Dalian, May 14 (Reporter Yang Yi), the 115,000-ton large-scale luxury international cruise ship "Selina" of the Costa Cruises originated from the Dalian Port International Airport Cruise Center of Liaoning Port Group on the 14th and went to Japan. A 5-day, 6-night cruise. This is the originating international cruise ship with the largest tonnage and the largest number of single-board passengers since the opening of the Dalian Port International Cruise Center in 2016.


The "Selina" has a total tonnage of 114,500 tons, with a passenger capacity of 3,780 and a deck of 14 floors.


Costa Serena is part of the largest cruise brand of the world's largest cruise ship group, Carnival Cruises. The first service in 2015 was in the Asian market. It was renovated in 2017. The total tonnage is 114,500 tons, the passenger capacity is 3,780, and the deck is 14 floors. The whole ship is decorated in ancient Roman style, with rich interior design and exquisite decoration. The details are perfect to create a city of “Ancient Rome” with a European romantic atmosphere and deep cultural roots.


According to reports, Dalian Port International Cruise Center has a 150,000-ton cruise berth and more than 8,000 square meters of passenger joint inspection waiting hall, which can meet the market demand for "big cruise" hardware. In order to do a good job in the terminal service of the "Selina", the Dalian Municipal Government Port Office led Dalian Port Passenger Transport Corporation, port joint inspection units, foreign shipping fleets, travel agencies and other parties, tailored to the characteristics of the "Selina" Operational plans for passenger assembly, baggage check-in and personnel clearance.

　Liaoning Port Group said that in September this year, Costa Cruises will also send its 57,000-ton luxury luxury cruise series "New Romance" to Dalian to start the cooperation between the Costa Cruises and Dalian. In the future, Liaoning Port Group will work with cruise lines companies, joint inspection orders and other departments to jointly build Dalian into an international cruise tourism center in Northeast Asia.
