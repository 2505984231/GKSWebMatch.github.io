Global shipping center city comprehensive strength ranking released

On July 11, 2019, the Xinhua・Baltic International Shipping Center Development Index was released simultaneously in the world. Singapore, Hong Kong, London, Shanghai and Dubai ranked the top five in the global international shipping center, and Ningbo-Zhoushan ranked 13th.

China's coastal cities have performed well in the global shipping center evaluation system. There are 10 cities (urban groups) in the top 30 of comprehensive strength, and others are Guangzhou (16), Qingdao (17), and Dalian (20). , Shenzhen (22), Tianjin (24), Kaohsiung (25), Xiamen (30).

In 2018, Ningbo Zhoushan Port maintained its global throughput of more than 1 billion tons of port status, while container throughput exceeded 26 million TEU for the first time, and ranked second in China with a strong growth rate of 7.07%. The third in the world.

The Asia-Pacific Port Development Report (2018) released by the Asia-Pacific Port Service Organization (APSN) believes that the growth of container throughput in Ningbo Zhoushan Port is mainly due to the business cooperation with the three major shipping alliances in route layout and hinterland expansion. . By the end of 2018, Ningbo Zhoushan Port had 246 routes of various types, including 120 trunk lines, accounting for nearly 50% of the total routes. Relying on the integrated development of Zhejiang's marine ports, Ningbo Zhoushan Port has accelerated the operation of branch transit and sea-river intermodal transportation. The feeder lines of Jiaxing Port, Wenzhou Port, Taizhou Port and Yiwu Port have been fed to the port for a large number of container sources, which greatly promoted Container throughput jumped.

The sea-rail combined transport business has also become an important boost to the container throughput growth of Zhoushan Port in Ningbo. In 2018, Ningbo Zhoushan Port opened nine sea-rail combined transport lines, increased the frequency of existing trains, and opened China's first double-deck container sea-rail intermodal train. The hinterland of the box source continued to extend to the inland areas. At present, there are 7 sea-rail combined transport lines with a monthly container volume of more than 5000 TEU in Zhoushan Port of Ningbo. Among them, the monthly business volume of Yiwu Bangui has normalized over 10,000 TEU, making it the largest sea-rail intermodal train in the country.

　Shipping and related economic activities are important indicators for measuring the connection between a city and the global economy. In recent years, the city has deepened its mutual benefit and win-win cooperation with countries along the “Belt and Road” and continued to optimize the open and efficient port business environment, in terms of cargo throughput, container throughput, smart port construction, shipping service capabilities, etc. Significant results have been achieved. The 13th Party Congress of Ningbo City held in February 2017 proposed a grand blueprint for the construction of an international port city. Ningbo Zhoushan Port also accelerated the process of building an international shipping hub port.

Mark Jackson, CEO of Baltic Exchange, said that this year's index report provides an in-depth analysis of the impact of the “Belt and Road Initiative” on the shipping industry. As the volume of import and export trade between Asia and neighboring countries increases, China's coastal large port cities are expected to attract more and more. More and more shipping companies.

Since 2014, the Xinhua・Baltic International Shipping Center Development Index has been released for six consecutive years. It reflects the comprehensive development level of international shipping center cities from three dimensions: port conditions, shipping services and comprehensive environment.

In the first five months of this year, the container throughput of Ningbo Zhoushan Port increased steadily, with a total of 11.49 million TEUs, a year-on-year increase of 4%.

Since the beginning of the year, Ningbo Zhoushan Port has been striving to improve the service level of the port and further cooperate with major shipping alliances to promote the “Economic Speed and Excellent Service” project. In the first 5 months, it has benefited more than 450 ships, saving fuel costs for shipping companies over 10 million. The US dollar; speeding up the “paperless” reform of the port, became the first “paperless” port for container import and export in the country at the end of April, and realized the “maximum run once” of the container import and export terminal business.

At the same time, Ningbo Zhoushan Port further improved the efficiency of port collection and distribution by expanding container yards, improving dock berthing capacity and supplementing intelligent equipment. This year, the first single-station hydro-transfer coal into the “transferred iron” business was completed, which introduced a new model for the transformation and development of port-based railway bulk cargo transportation.
