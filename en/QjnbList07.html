Biography

●Mao Yisheng (January 9, 1896 - November 12, 1989), the word Tang Chen, Zhenjiang, Jiangsu, graduated from Carnegie Institute of Technology (now Carnegie Mellon University), Chinese civil engineering expert, bridge expert , engineering educator, academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, academician of the American Academy of Engineering, academician of the Academia Sinica, has made outstanding contributions to the advancement of railway science and technology. He once presided over the construction of the first modern large-scale bridge designed and built by the Chinese, the Qiantang River Bridge. After the founding of New China, he participated in the design of the Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge. On November 12, 1989, he died in Beijing at the age of 93.


Historical life

Mao Yisheng’s grandfather, Mao Qian, was the founder of the Nanyang Guanbao and was a famous person in Zhenjiang City. Shortly after Mao Yisheng was born, the family moved to Nanjing. Mao Yisheng grew up from a young age and was good at thinking independently.


At the age of 6, he studied privately. At the age of 7 he studied at the first new primary school in China, founded in 1903, the Siyi School, and entered the Jiangnan Business School in 1905.


When Mao Yisheng was 10 years old, after the Dragon Boat Festival, the dragon boat race was held in his hometown. The people watching the game were standing on Wende Bridge, and he did not go because of stomach pain. On the bridge, many people collapsed the bridge, drowning and drowning many people. This unfortunate incident was heavily pressed in Mao Yisheng’s heart. He made up his mind: When he grows up, he must make the strongest bridge. From then on, Mao Yisheng only saw the bridge, whether it was a stone bridge or a wooden bridge, he always looked at the bridge from the bridge to the bridge. After Mao Yisheng went to school, he read the articles and passages about the bridge from the books, and copied it on the book. When he encountered the picture about the bridge, he clipped it up. It took a long time to accumulate a few thick books.


In 1916, he graduated from Tangshan Industrial College of the Ministry of Communications (Tangshan Jiaotong University, now Southwest Jiaotong University). Participate in the postgraduate examination of Tsinghua University in the United States, and take the first list to stay in the ocean.


In 1917, he obtained a master's degree (bridge major) from Cornell University.


In 1919, he received his Ph.D. from Carnegie Institute of Technology (now Carnegie Mellon University) and was the first doctor of engineering at the school.


After graduating from the Tangshan Industrial College of the Ministry of Communications in 1916, he was sent to the United States for study by the Tsinghua Academy. In 1917, he obtained a master's degree in civil engineering from Cornell University, USA, and was awarded the "Fiutis" Gold Research Medal by Cornell University. Because of the excellent performance of the company, Cornell University received the graduates of Tangshan Jiaotong University* (now Southwest Jiaotong University) and won honors for Chinese universities, especially the alma mater. In 1921, he received a doctorate in engineering from the University of Carnegie Mellon University. The scientific creation of his doctoral thesis "Second Stress of Bridge Truss" is called "Mao's Law" and won the Gold Medal Medal for the outstanding graduate student of Cornell University.


After returning to China in 1920 to 1930, he served as professor and deputy director of Tangshan School of Jiaotong University (now Southwest Jiaotong University), professor of National Southeast University, director of engineering, president of Nanjing Hehai University of Engineering, and president of Tangshan University (now Southwest Jiaotong University) of the Ministry of Communications .


From 1930 to 1931, he served as the director of the Jiangsu Water Conservancy Bureau. From 1934 to 1937, he served as the director of the Zhejiang Qiantang River Bridge Engineering Office (who was in charge until 1949). He presided over the design and organized a construction on the Qiantang River, where the natural conditions were more complicated. The two-way highway and railway dual-use Qiantang River Bridge with a total length of 1,453 meters and a foundation depth of 47.8 meters. The bridge was completed and opened to traffic on September 26, 1937. This is the first modern steel bridge designed and constructed by the Chinese. It is an immortal monument in the history of Chinese bridge engineering. On December 23, 1937, in order to prevent the Japanese army from attacking Hangzhou, Mao Yisheng personally participated in the bombing bridge. After the victory of the Anti-Japanese War, Mao Yisheng was ordered to organize the restoration of the bridge. In March 1948, the bridge was repaired and opened to traffic.


From 1937 to 1942, he served as dean and dean of Tangshan Institute of Engineering (now Southwest Jiaotong University) of National Chiao Tung University.


From 1942 to 1943, he served as Director of the Bridge Design and Engineering Division of the Ministry of Communications.


From 1943 to 1949, he served as general manager of China Bridge Corporation.


In 1943, he was elected professor of the Ministry of Education of the Republic of China.


In 1948, he was elected a member of the Academia Sinica.


From 1949 to 1952, he was appointed President of China Jiaotong University (renamed Northern Jiaotong University in 1950), which was established by his alma mater Tangshan Institute of Technology (now Southwest Jiaotong University) and Peking Railway Management College (now Beijing Jiaotong University).


From 1951 to 1981, he served as director of the Railway Technology Research Institute and dean of the Railway Science Research Institute.


In 1955, he was elected as a member of the Chinese Academy of Sciences (member of the Faculty).


In 1959, in the construction of Beijing's top ten buildings, he served as the head of the structure review team of the Great Hall of the People, and appointed the person in charge of the final design of the design plan for Premier Zhou Enlai.


From 1955 to 1957, he designed the Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge. From 1955 to 1957, Mao Yisheng was appointed as the chairman of the Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge Technical Advisory Committee. He also accepted the task of building the first bridge across the Yangtze River, the Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge.


In 1977, he designed the Chongqing Shibanpo Yangtze River Bridge. In 1982, he was elected a foreign academician of the National Academy of Engineering.


In 1984, he was elected honorary chairman of the China Association for Science and Technology.


He died in Beijing on November 12, 1989, at the age of 93.
