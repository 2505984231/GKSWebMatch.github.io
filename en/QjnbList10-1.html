Biography

● Fang Qinhan (1925.04.20-2014.10.14) expert in bridge engineering. People in Huangyan District, Taizhou City, Zhejiang Province. In 1950, he graduated from the Department of Civil Engineering of Tsinghua University with a bachelor's degree. From 1954 to 1958, he served as the person in charge of the design of Xiangjiang Bridge and Wujiang Bridge, and the deputy chief engineer of Chongqing Baishatuo Yangtze River Bridge. From 1958 to 2004, he presided over the design of steel beams for national key bridges such as Nanjing Yangtze River Bridge, Jiujiang Yangtze River Bridge and Wuhu Yangtze River Bridge. And the person in charge of research. According to the development plan of the railway bridge of the Ministry of Railways, combined with the actual project, develop, innovate and promote a variety of new materials, new structures and new processes, in order to realize the sustainable development of railway steel bridges and realize the railway bridge "high strength, large span, light weight, overall "The technical policy has made a contribution. He has won five national science and technology progress awards and several provincial and ministerial level science and technology awards. In 1997, he was elected as an academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.


Personal achievements

He has published three exchange papers abroad and more than 10 papers published in China. Since joining the work, he has been engaged in the design and research, promotion, research and development of the steel beam of the bridge for a long time, and has innovated a variety of new materials, new structures and new processes, so that the bridge projects designed and built have reached the international advanced level in the same period, in order to realize the railway. The bridge's development goals of “high strength, large span, light weight and overall” have made outstanding contributions. He has won five awards for scientific and technological progress and six awards for scientific and technological progress of the Ministry of Railways.


He was involved in the discussion and revision of the railway bridge and culvert specification of the Ministry of Railways (the steel structure part of the 1959 edition). It was the main writer of the second bridge culvert (steel structure part) of the 1975 edition of the railway engineering technical specification; in the 1980s, he participated in the structural reliability specification of the Ministry of Railways. Prepared as a member of the editorial board and the main drafter; in the 1990s, he edited the bridge steel structure design specification and was the main drafter. In 1987, he was awarded the title of “Model Worker” in Hubei Province. In 1992, he received special government allowance. In December 1997, he was elected as an academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.
