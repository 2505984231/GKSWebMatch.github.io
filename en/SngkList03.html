Kong Qingfen

Kong Qingfen, the female captain of the first ocean-going ship in New China, was the first female captain of the ocean-going ship cultivated in New China. Although she is old and young, she has worked hard for nearly 10,000 days and nights in the vast sea, and has made outstanding contributions to the navigation of the motherland, and has been admired and remembered. In the early 1950s, Kong Qingfen was elected as a representative of the Tianjin Maritime Staff Association due to his outstanding performance and went to Beijing to participate in the National Women's Representative Conference.

Personal experience

During his nautical career, Kong Qingfen has driven a total of 28 different types of ships of 3,000 to 60,000 tons, maneuvering various types of main engines such as steam turbines, internal combustion engines and turbines, sailing through the Bohai Sea, Zhoushan Sea, Pacific Japan Sea, Tokyo. The waters of the Bay, the Taiwan Strait and Hong Kong; have independently manipulated many types of ships such as freighters, passenger ships and oil tankers, and have driven various types of ships ranging from 1,000 tons to 10,000 tons. She sailed across the Bohai Sea, sailed across the Yellow Sea, the East China Sea and the South China Sea. She sailed from Shanghai to Japan, and she was familiar with the route from Shanghai to Hong Kong.Hundreds of farewells and return to the large and small seaports of the motherland.

Honors

In 1954, Kong Qingfen was 21 years old and passed the examination to obtain the three certificates of the ship. In 1968, he was promoted to the rank of chief officer. In 1969, he was officially appointed as the captain by the captain’s technical appraisal test. In 1974, Kong Qingfen was the captain of a 10,000-ton tanker. In 1976, Kong Qingfen obtained the certificate of the first-class captain of the Ocean Shipping and became the first female ocean-going captain trained by China. Since then, the Chinese nation has sailed the first female navigator. Kong Qingfen drove the “Feng Tao” ocean liner to Japan and was warmly praised by friends of the Moon. In 1980, the "Shanghai-Hong Kong" route was reopened after more than 30 years of suspension. As the captain, she pointed to the passenger ship port between the mainland and Hong Kong.

Contribution

Kong Qingfen has been working in the maritime career of the motherland to retire. The first female captain of the new China dedicated her youth and mind to the sea. She regards driving ocean-going vessels to communicate the friendly exchanges of people of all countries as the greatest glory and happiness. She has made outstanding contributions to the cause of sailing in New China. For her career, she has never married, and dedicated her life to the navigation of the motherland. When Kong Qingfen was over 50 years old, she always smiled and said to good-hearted people: I am the first female seafarer trained by the party. As long as my body is still suitable for navigation, I will continue to work at sea. At 3:09 am on May 28, 2005, Kong Qingfen died in Tianjin at the age of 73. China and the people regret the death of a great navigator like her.
