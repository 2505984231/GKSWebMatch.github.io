Dalian Port

Introduction

Dalian Port is located in the center of the Northwest Pacific Ocean, at the southern end of Dalian Bay. The geographical coordinates are 38°55′44′′ north latitude and 121°39′17′′ east longitude. It is the center of the emerging Northeast Asian economic circle. It is the region that enters the Pacific Ocean and faces the world. Maritime portal.

Dalian Port As of 2015, Dalian Port has a water area of 346 square kilometers, a core port area of about 18 square kilometers, a land area of 15 square kilometers, a bonded port area of 6.88 square kilometers, and a container handling capacity of 16 million TEUs. . In 2017, Dalian Port achieved cargo throughput of 451 million tons.

Dalian Port

history
In 1899, Dalian Port began construction; in 1951, Dalian Port was officially handed over to the Central People's Government of the People's Republic of China by the Soviet Army; on July 28, 2005, Dalian Port Laogang District began to relocate. On April 12, 2019, it was selected by the China Science and Technology Coordination Propaganda Department, the China Association for Science and Technology Innovation Strategy Research Institute, and the China Urban Planning Association to jointly host the “China Industrial Heritage Protection List (Second Batch)”.
business
Dalian Port is principally engaged in the business of loading, unloading, storage and transportation of crude oil, refined oil and liquid chemicals, and passenger transportation services. The operation scope of Dalian Port mainly includes Dagang, Xianglu Reef, Dalian Bay and Dayao Bay. In addition to the railway collection and distribution tasks of major specialized terminal companies in the port, it is also responsible for the new shipbuilding plant, the second gas, Beihai Power Plant and Xianghai. Railway vehicles such as power plants and Donghai Power Plants take delivery operations.
Geographical advantage
Dalian Port is the center of the northwestern Pacific Ocean. It is the center of the emerging Northeast Asian economic circle. It is the maritime gateway of the region that enters the Pacific Ocean and faces the world. The port has a wide and deep water, no silt and no freezing, and its natural conditions are very advantageous. It is the most convenient port for transshipment of goods from the Far East, South Asia, North America and Europe.
